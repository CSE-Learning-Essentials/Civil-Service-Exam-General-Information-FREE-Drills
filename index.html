SECTION 4: GENERAL INFORMATION

PART 1: PHILIPPINE CONSTITUTION

PASSING SCORE: 8

Directions: Choose the correct answer from the choices given.

1. Who has the exclusive power to initiate all cases of impeachment under the 1987 Philippine Constitution?

2. Under the Constitution, how many consecutive terms may a Senator serve?

3. Which Constitutional body is tasked to safeguard the integrity of elections?

4. According to the Constitution, what is the minimum age requirement to run for Senator?

5. Which Article of the Constitution declares that the Philippines adopts and pursues an independent foreign policy?

6. The right of the people to information on matters of public concern is found under which Article?

7. What is the maximum period within which the President must submit the proposed national budget to Congress after the opening of its regular session?

8. The Constitution prohibits which of the following punishments?

9. What is the minimum voting requirement of the Supreme Court to declare a law unconstitutional?

10. Who succeeds the President if both the President and Vice President are unable to serve?

Correct Answers & Explanation:

  1. C — House of Representatives (Art. XI, Sec. 3(1))
  2. B — 2 terms (Art. VI, Sec. 4)
  3. D — Commission on Elections (Art. IX-C)
  4. C — 35 years old (Art. VI, Sec. 3)
  5. B — Article II (Sec. 7)
  6. B — Article III (Sec. 7)
  7. C — 30 days (Art. VII, Sec. 22)
  8. B — Death penalty (Art. III, Sec. 19)
  9. A — Majority of all members (Art. VIII, Sec. 4(2))
  10. A — Senate President (Art. VII, Sec. 7)
