Section 4: General Information - Part 1: Philippine Constitution

Passing Score: 8/10

Directions: Choose the correct answer from the choices given.

1. As provided under Article I of the 1987 Philippine Constitution, the following comprise the national territory of the Philippines except:

  • A) internal waters
  • B) the Philippine Archipelago
  • C) high or open seas
  • D) territorial sea
  • E) terrestrial, fluvial or aerial domain

2. The Philippines is considered an archipelago because:

  • A) it is a state composed of many islands.
  • B) it is surrounded by an extensive body of water.
  • C) it has three islands known as Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao.
  • D) the waters around it from part of its internal waters.
  • E) its island groups are regarded as separate units with their own territorial seas.

3. How are we as Filipino people described in the preamble?

  • A) resilient
  • B) tenacious
  • C) sovereign
  • D) freedom-loving
  • E) just and humane

4. _____ refers to the basic guidelines or direction followed by a government of a country to promote its national interest with respect to its affairs with other countries.

  • A) State policy
  • B) Internal policy
  • C) Domestic policy
  • D) Foreign policy
  • E) Independent policy

5. What does the principle "civilian authority is, at all times, supreme over the military" signify under the 1987 Philippine Constitution?

  • A) The Armed Forces of the Philippines has control over civil government during emergencies
  • B) Civilian and military authorities share equal roles in democratic governance
  • C) Military officers have equal power with civilian officials in policymaking
  • D) The military can override civilian decisions when national security is at stake
  • E) The President, as civilian commander-in-chief, exercises authority over the military

6. Who is the head of state and head of government under the 1987 Philippine Constitution?

  • A) Chief Justice
  • B) Senate President
  • C) Speaker of the House
  • D) President
  • E) Vice President

7. Which branch of government is responsible for interpreting the laws under the 1987 Philippine Constitution?

  • A) Executive
  • B) Legislative
  • C) Administrative
  • D) Electoral
  • E) Judiciary

8. How many justices are there in the Supreme Court of the Philippines?

  • A) 14
  • B) 9
  • C) 11
  • D) 15
  • E) 21

9. What branch of the government has the vested power to make, alter and repeal laws?

  • A) Supreme Court
  • B) Office of the President
  • C) Congress
  • D) Constitutional Commission
  • E) Local Government

10. Which article of the 1987 Philippine Constitution deals with the Legislative Department?

  • A) Article I
  • B) Article II
  • C) Article V
  • D) Article VI
  • E) Article VII
